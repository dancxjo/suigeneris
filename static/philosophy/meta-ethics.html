Meta-Ethics

Meta-ethics delves into the very nature of ethical judgments and concepts. It asks questions like: What does it mean for something to be morally good or bad? Are moral truths objective, or are they subjective?

Subjectivism vs. Objectivism

Two prominent schools of thought in meta-ethics are subjectivism and objectivism.

  • Subjectivism: Ethical judgments are based on personal feelings or opinions.
  • Objectivism: There are objective moral truths that exist independently of human opinion.

Further Exploration

  • Normative Ethics: Focuses on what actions are morally right and wrong.
  • Applied Ethics: Addresses specific ethical issues in various fields, such as medicine, business, and technology.
  • Logic: The study of valid reasoning and argumentation, crucial for ethical analysis.
