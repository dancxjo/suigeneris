Normative Ethics

Welcome to Suigeneris's exploration of normative ethics! This branch of philosophy grapples with questions of right and wrong, good and bad, and how we should live our lives.

What is Normative Ethics?

At its core, normative ethics seeks to establish moral standards and principles that guide our actions and judgments. It asks fundamental questions like:

  • What makes an action morally right or wrong?
  • What are our obligations to others?
  • How should we make decisions in complex moral dilemmas?

Different Ethical Frameworks

There are various ethical frameworks that offer different perspectives on these questions. Some prominent ones include:

  • Utilitarianism: Emphasizes maximizing happiness and well-being for the greatest number of people.
  • Deontology: Focuses on duty, principles, and universal moral rules.
  • Virtue Ethics: Emphasizes cultivating virtuous character traits and living a morally good life.
