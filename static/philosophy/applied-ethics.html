Applied Ethics

Ethics is a branch of philosophy that deals with moral principles and values. Applied ethics takes these principles and applies them to real-world situations.

Areas of Applied Ethics

  • Business Ethics: Examines ethical issues in the workplace, such as corporate responsibility, fair labor practices, and environmental sustainability.
  • Bioethics: Focuses on ethical dilemmas in medicine, healthcare, and biotechnology, including genetic engineering, cloning, and end-of-life care.
  • Environmental Ethics: Addresses our moral obligations to the environment and explores issues such as pollution, climate change, and animal welfare.
  • Political Ethics: Deals with ethical principles in government, politics, and law, including justice, equality, and human rights.
